IV. Industry Catalysts (Top Three Themes This Episode)

⚡ Passive Components: Resistors Follow Through
A new round of price hikes expected in early July. The initial view was conservative (resistor utilization lagged capacitors/inductors), but the rally has become "unstoppable." Large EMS/ODM buyers have accepted capacitor price hikes; procurement teams, fearing supply disruptions, are now over-ordering resistors → supply/demand tightens.
→ Key beneficiary: Yageo (strongest chart pattern; barely pulled back during the correction)
🤖 ASIC Supply-Chain Expansion
MediaTek is developing Trigger Fish for Google (an enhanced version of the existing chip — very early stage but progressing well); Broadcom's W-Fish 9th-gen dual-chip (confirmed not skipped) is a positive for substrate suppliers. MediaTek + Marvell have already carved out market share in ASICs.
→ Beneficiaries: MediaTek, Broadcom, substrate suppliers
🔌 Power Components: Capacity Is the Bottleneck
Continuing the EP672 theme: power components remain constrained by capacity. Only companies with stable 8-inch wafer capacity or vertical foundry capability have meaningful upside.
→ Watch: Power component players with captive capacity or foundry capability

V. Market Outlook

📊 "Long bull rest," not a false breakout: A pullback after a sharp rally is a normal correction; a false breakout is when the market consolidates for a long time, pops briefly, then immediately crashes through the floor.
🇰🇷 Korea Effect: The trigger is suspected to be Korea-related — Korean retail investors love leveraged ETFs and certain US stocks (Tesla, Palantir); a forced-liquidation cascade can weigh on global sentiment, analogous to the Bill Hwang (Archegos) collapse.
🔄 Positioning: Considered rotating from small/mid-caps into TSMC as a hedge, but found that small/mid-caps "hadn't died completely" — the market hadn't entered a "junk tape" where only mega-caps move — so positions were rotated back.
