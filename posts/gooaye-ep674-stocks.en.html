II. Recent Market Interpretation (Late-June Correction)

📊 Primary driver is Rebalancing: sovereign wealth funds, pension funds, and other large funds mechanically selling outperforming tech stocks at quarter-end to restore target allocations — a normal occurrence; more concrete than invoking "war" or "rate hikes."
🛢️ Using oil prices to rebut the war narrative: argues that low oil prices indicate the market believes Middle East tensions are fading, and IRGC-related news is mostly disinformation. (Fact-check: WTI was actually ~$88–92, not the $70–80 cited — this undermines the premise of the argument.)
📈 Low probability of rate hikes: price increases are concentrated in Data Centers, and demand destruction on the consumer side has not yet spread into broad inflation.
⚠️ Panic margin-call selling: June futures hit limit-down first, then intraday forced liquidations on the Taiwan market — judged as high-leverage investors being forcibly unwound, not a fundamental deterioration. However, some popular groups have broken below the 60-day moving average; everyone who bought in the past 60 days is underwater, creating heavy overhead supply on any bounce and making a re-test of highs unlikely.

III. AI Capex & Application-Layer Rotation

The host worries that big-tech shareholders are growing impatient with massive Capex that has yet to be reflected in share prices — a repeat of the DeepSeek-driven scrutiny wave. He believes capital should rotate next into the AI application layer, and favors Google (excellent cost-cutting execution, comprehensive model deployment).

Taiwan supply-chain order numbers show no obvious anomalies, suggesting this selloff is a temporary loss of market sentiment — similar to the two earlier corrections in March and early June.

IV. Apple Pricing Issue

Under memory price-hike pressure, Apple has raised prices on its existing product lines (MacBook, etc.) by approximately 10–20%. The host's view:

🍎 This is a warm-up for new-product price hikes — official new products may be even pricier.
👥 Core customers (Apple loyalists) are price-insensitive — they and the "general public who finds it expensive" occupy completely different circles; sales won't be affected.
🤖 Apple still has the opportunity to lead in Edge-side AI, but has yet to find a compelling entry point — waiting for a turn-strength signal.

VII. Objective Commentary

Sound Arguments

The Rebalance thesis is a logical and commonly observed framework in mature markets — more concrete than war or rate hikes. The 60-day moving average as a risk gauge has historical backing; the overhead-supply logic after a breach is sound. AI capital rotation to the application layer is a widely discussed, reasonable hypothesis. Apple brand-stickiness thesis is supported by high repurchase rates and ecosystem lock-in data.

Caveats

Micron gross margin "80-something%": Futu shows FY2026 Q2 GAAP gross margin at 74.4%, short of 80%. If citing non-GAAP, the source should be stated; simply saying "80-something%" is imprecise.

Oil price "$70–80": WTI was actually ~$88–92, a clear discrepancy — and this figure directly underpins his "market no longer fears war" thesis, making the error non-trivial.

0050 "roughly 50%": actual YTD is ~57% — his estimate was conservative, diluting the impact; a 57% half-year return on a market-cap-weighted ETF is a historic result.

Overall: this episode centers on personal trading reflections and macro impressions. The two clear numeric discrepancies (oil price, Micron gross margin) appear to be imprecise recall rather than deliberate distortion. No obvious pump-and-dump language or cherry-picking, but his concentrated, high-turnover style is unsuitable for most retail investors — listeners should not treat it as investment advice.
