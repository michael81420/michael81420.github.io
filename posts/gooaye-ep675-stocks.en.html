TL;DR · What this episode covers

🗓️ Market tempo: H1 window-dressing and foreign rebalancing are done — a strong limit-up run on 6/30 (settlement day) suggests July faces less pressure.

🔩 Theme 1: Passive-component price hikes — MLCC / resistors enter the "revenue-realization" phase; Taiwanese/Japanese makers roll out hike notices from July, likened to the early-mid stage of the memory cycle.

🔌 Theme 2: MOSFET shortage — Western/Japanese Tier-1 IDMs lack capacity for high-voltage / AI-server parts; Taiwanese Tier-2 makers pick up overflow orders.

🤖 Theme 3: IC-design rebound — MediaTek / GUC / Alchip reclaim key moving averages, capital rotating back in, driven by AI ASIC demand.

📏 Screening bar: "If YoY isn't 50%, you're nothing" — a forward verification bar, but most named names haven't hit it yet.

★ This episode leans on industry trends and supply-chain chatter, much of it forward-looking calls and private-channel intel. The fact-check table below flags what's backed by news/financials vs. what has no public source (MediaTek's TPU target, the MOSFET shortage).

1 · July tape: foreign rebalancing wraps up

🗓️ H1 settlement closes out: a strong limit-up run on 6/30 suggests foreign rebalancing and window-dressing "largely wrapped," easing July's tape. (A macro-tempo call, no verifiable number — track from here.)
📏 Higher screening bar: "If YoY isn't 50%, you're nothing" — a bar set for future verification, not a claim that his holdings already clear it.
🧭 Trading discipline: wait for consensus to converge before entering, don't chase; cut the weak, keep the strong — some names never recover, so be prepared mentally. (Anti-hype, consistent with momentum-investing logic.)

2 · Passive-component price-hike wave

From July, Taiwanese/Japanese makers will roll out hike notices, spilling over to non-AI EOL parts and Tier-2 makers. He frames the move as "like memory, at an early-mid stage." Names MLCC / capacitor plays.

📈 "Revenue-realization" phase: Yageo ran from 230 to 1,055 over 6 months (+358%) — a big move — yet monthly revenue YoY has only just approached 50%. Fundamentals are catching up while valuation ran ahead; "early-mid stage" is a fair characterization.
⚠️ But note: Walsin Tech's monthly revenue YoY is only +16.5%, showing the "revenue-realization" is truly just starting; the hikes aren't fully in the financials yet.

3 · MOSFET / power-component shortage

The supply chain reports MOSFET lead times and prices rising across the board; what's short is Western/Japanese Tier-1 IDM high-voltage and AI-server parts, with Taiwanese Tier-2 makers taking overflow orders. He likens it to "Tier-2 makers eating their fill," echoing the DDR4/5-beats-HBM overflow logic.

🔌 Demand-driven > cost pass-through: he stresses demand-driven hikes beat cost pass-through. (But most power-component makers are still shortage/cost stories — monthly revenue YoY in single digits — with no demand-explosion financials yet.)
❓ Shortage has no public data: rising lead times are private-channel intel, unverifiable with public data; Fu Sheng's +95.2% over one month is circumstantial, not verification.

4 · IC-design rebound & AI ASIC

He says IC-design leaders reclaimed key moving averages with capital rotating back, driven by AI ASIC demand. Names MediaTek (market talk of a Google TPU 2026 target of US$1B), GUC (ASIC design services), and Alchip (3nm/2nm AI accelerators).

📉 "Above the MA" needs a split view: MediaTek is actually down -4.0% over one month (4,525 → 4,345) — weak here; GUC +17.5% and Alchip +7.0% are the ones that are strong. Can't generalize.
❓ The TPU US$1B target isn't findable: MediaTek hasn't disclosed this figure; it's a market estimate / analyst extrapolation, not to be cited as fact.

7 · Objective take

Worth taking on board

The "early-mid stage" call on passives is data-reasonable: Yageo's share price has run a lot (+358%) but monthly revenue YoY only approaches 50% — fundamentals realizing while valuation ran first, so "early-mid stage" fits. "Wait for consensus to converge, don't chase" is anti-hype momentum discipline; "cut the weak, keep the strong — some never recover" is mature risk management.

Keep a grain of salt

Power components — valuation detached from fundamentals: Fu Sheng +95.2% and Sanyo Semi +29.0% over one month, but PE already 56–144x while monthly revenue YoY is single digits to 24% — most are still shortage/cost stories, no demand explosion yet.

Alchip is pure narrative: monthly revenue YoY -33.5%, yet the stock is +97% off its low — entirely on the AI ASIC story; if order visibility is low, it's the highest-risk name.

MediaTek's TPU US$1B: no public source, narrative-level, not to be treated as fact.

Most miss the 50% bar: of the named names, only GUC (+132.1%) clears it, Yageo (+47.5%) is close, the other 8 are well below. The verification node is July–August monthly revenue and earnings calls; if the hikes don't show in Q3 financials, this basket has correction risk.

Overall: the narrative framework is logically sound and the industry direction (hikes, shortage, ASIC) has news support, but this episode leans on forward calls and supply-chain chatter, not public numbers. For names already up a lot (Fu Sheng, Walsin Tech, Yageo), entering now is chasing — have a clear stop-loss plan.
