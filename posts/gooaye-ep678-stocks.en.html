2 · Meta renting out idle compute: doubling down vs exiting

The market briefly read Meta selling/renting idle GPUs as "exiting the AI race." The host pushes back: it's a strategy to monetize old Hopper capacity, and it actually proves GPUs are "an asset that can earn money" — which serves as a counter to Michael Burry's depreciation critique (Burry argues hyperscalers stretch GPU depreciation schedules too far, underbooking expense to flatter earnings). But the host also notes this is a negative for pure-rental NeoClouds (e.g. CoreWeave): a super-buyer with pricing power now competes with them, and the sky-high "dream multiples" propped up by a buying queue face a stress test.

✅ The narrative, timing and market reaction all match real news: media in early July did report Meta building "Meta Compute" to sell idle capacity; on the news Meta jumped while NeoCloud/GPU indices sold off in tandem. Burry's depreciation critique is also real (he accuses several hyperscalers of stretching GPU depreciation to 5~6 years, underbooking a cumulative ~$176B of expense across 2026~2028, and has shorted Nvidia).
⚠️ It adds a counter-argument, it doesn't refute Burry: that a GPU can be rented out doesn't make a longer depreciation schedule correct — that's still an accounting judgment, and Burry's short thesis isn't overturned. The host was restrained here, not claiming Burry got "owned." Meta's latest price is $669.21, forward PE 21.2x (20.8th valuation percentile — cheap relative to itself).

3 · The price-hike cycle: a broad "surface" spread across the chain

The second core thread: hike and shortage signals are showing up across the whole chain almost simultaneously — ADI sent a price-increase notice, memory contract prices keep rising (with the pace narrowing), passives are hiking broadly ("mother hen leading the chicks"), power devices/MOSFETs are short, lead-frame lead times stretched from 3~4 months to 7~8 and rose 10~20% (tracking copper), and packaging houses are at full capacity with earnings jumping. The host frames this as a macro "surface" shift in pricing power — more informative than any single company's earnings — all tracing back to AI datacenter buildout squeezing supply of legacy components.

Direction is fully correct. ADI latest $395.65, forward PE 36.4x.

4 · The optical "narrow-fast vs wide-slow" route war + the host's holdings

Inside the optical group there's a route war: silicon photonics + indium phosphide (Narrow and Fast, chasing peak bandwidth for long-distance scale-out) has looked technically weak lately; VCSEL + AOC (Wide and Slow, stacking cheap mature consumer-electronics parts for aggregate bandwidth over short in-rack scale-up distances) has traded firmer, with capital rotating within an already richly-valued optical group. The host frames this not as a zero-sum "who replaces whom" but as a division of use cases — echoing his long-standing "copper and light coexist" view.

He mentions his holdings Credo (CRDO) and Astera Labs (ALAB) were mispriced lower in the "light-replaces-copper" panic selling, and says both are now at "new highs," to argue fundamentals-driven investors needn't be spooked by narrative shifts; he adds that the true "pure copper play" is nVent (NVT), while CRDO/ALAB are just associative.

⚠️ "CRDO/ALAB both at new highs" clearly conflicts with the latest close: CRDO last closed $257.79, down ~16.5% from its 6/22 high of $308.67; ALAB last closed $412.97, down ~17.3% from its 6/30 high of $499.48 — both are pulling back from highs, not at new highs. Over ~14 months they are indeed up several-fold from the lows, but "at new highs now" is a stale/overstated claim.
⚠️ ALAB is valued on PS, not PE: Futu uses PS 70.7x for ALAB (97.2nd percentile — expensive), implying GAAP earnings power isn't yet stable/representative — something the host didn't disclose. Listeners who only hear "new highs, fundamentals folks needn't worry" may underestimate the thin-earnings-visibility risk.
⚠️ NVT's business description is right, but it's not actually cheap: nVent genuinely does electrical connection/busbar "hardware copper," a different business model from CRDO/ALAB's high-speed signaling chips (which merely use copper cable as a medium) — so the distinction is fair. But NVT trades at trailing PE 53.2x (85.2nd percentile — expensive vs itself), forward 38.4x, even richer than CRDO/ALAB's forward multiples. "Purer" doesn't mean "cheaper" — a risk the host didn't mention.

7 · Objective take

What holds up

On the macro narratives the fact-checks almost all pass: Meta's move to rent out compute — the action, timing and market reaction (NeoClouds selling off) — matches real news closely; the ADI notice details are actually more precise than the video (tiered 10~30% hikes, effective Feb 1), and the whole-chain hike logic is reasonable; Michael Burry's depreciation critique genuinely exists and is unresolved, and the host merely offered a counter-argument rather than claiming to have refuted it — a restrained framing. What to keep in mind

"CRDO/ALAB are at new highs now" is inaccurate against the latest close: both are down 16~17% from late-June highs — only up hugely relative to the lows a year ago. This is classic "long-term story papering over a short-term pullback"; a listener who assumes chasing here is risk-free could be misled.

ALAB is valued on PS, not PE, reflecting still-unstable GAAP earnings power; the host didn't disclose this and only said "new highs, fundamentals folks needn't fear a pullback," which may lead people to underestimate the thin-earnings-visibility risk.

NVT as a "pure copper play" isn't actually cheap (trailing PE 85th percentile); the "purer business" logic holds, but "purer" ≠ "cheaper," and the host skipped the valuation risk.

Passives/packaging/lead-frames aren't named to tickers — classic Gooaye style, but it means this segment is more industry explainer than verifiable stock claim; the overall tone is uniformly bullish, even quoting 0050's return more conservatively than reality, which somewhat reinforces bullish conviction while omitting an "already up a lot, some names pulling back" risk note.
