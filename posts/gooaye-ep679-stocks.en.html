1. Korea's deleveraging: the real driver of this global pullback

His core thesis: the root of this global (incl. TW) risk-asset pullback isn't a macro narrative like inflation or war, but Korean retail's extreme leverage unwinding. The Korean index is extremely concentrated (huge semiconductor weight) and leverage products are rife, so a selloff triggers cascading forced liquidations, amplifying volatility to 2–3x other markets before spilling over into global deleveraging. This is the highest-value segment of the episode.

✅ True — even more severe than he says: KOSPI is down 27% from its June record high, fell nearly 9% in a single day on 7/13, tripping its 7th circuit breaker of the year (5 within two months); forced-liquidation over the first 10 days of July hit KRW 425.8bn (~NT$9.2bn), with KRW 142.2bn on 7/9 alone. His tone is relatively understated.
⚠️ "Korean leverage could be 6–10x+" is a subjective estimate: this comes from chats with a friend, with no official or institutional data to verify — readers shouldn't treat it as a precise statistic.

2. TW margin tightening: brokers pull lines, yet balance "only fell NT$10bn+"

He describes brokers tightening margin lines across the board (unrestricted-purpose loans, pledged loans, etc.), which in a selloff should crush margin balance; but on gut feel he said "I thought it'd fall NT$20–30bn+, but it only dropped NT$10bn+" — retail wasn't scared into mass deleveraging. That "should've dropped but didn't" resilience is, to him, a sign of bullish underpinning.

✅✅ Matches FinMind official data precisely: margin balance went from 618.3bn to 604.0bn on 7/13→7/14, down only ~NT$14.26bn, and even rebounded to 615.4bn on 7/15 (a "miracle bounce"). A gut-feel number landing this close to official data is the most commendable part of the episode.

❓ "Brokers tightening across the board" can't be quantified publicly: this is first-hand experience from him and nearby brokers — a reasonable market observation, but with no official data to verify directly; filed as "reasonable but not quantitatively verified."
