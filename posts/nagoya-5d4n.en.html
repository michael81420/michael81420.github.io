【Day 1】Local Faith & Subculture Experience

After arriving at Chubu Centrair International Airport, please collect your reserved train tickets at the airport first. Then take the Meitetsu Limited Express to Nagoya Station (approx. 35–40 min) and drop off your luggage at the hotel.

Atsuta Shrine (09:30–13:00)

  • Transportation: Take JR Tokaido Line or Meitetsu Main Line from Nagoya Station to Jingumae / Atsuta Station, approx. 10–15 min.
  • Highlights: One of Japan's three great shrines, enshrining the legendary Kusanagi sword. Lush woodland, serene atmosphere; Nobunaga's Wall is also a famous historical landmark.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Atsuta Horaiken Jingū-ten: A must-try hitsumabushi eel restaurant — rich charcoal-grilled aroma and three ways to eat.
  • Miya Kishimen Jingū-ten: A century-old eatery inside the shrine, serving Nagoya's signature flat udon noodles.
  • Kiyome Mochi Sōhonke: Post-visit sweet — silky soft mochi skin with smooth red bean paste filling.

Osu Kannon & Osu Shopping Street (13:30–18:00)

  • Transportation: From Atsuta Shrine take the Meijo subway line to Kamimaezu Station, or transfer to the Tsuruma Line to Osu Kannon Station, approx. 15–20 min.
  • Highlights: Known as "Nagoya's Akihabara" — packed with vintage clothing, second-hand electronics, and street food from around the world. Vibrant local atmosphere and incredibly fun to explore.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Yabaton Yabachō Honten: The ultimate miso katsu restaurant — sizzling iron-plate miso sauce, bold and satisfying.
  • Lee-san no Taiwan Meibutsu Yatai Osu Honten: Popular Taiwanese fried chicken stall — great for eating on the go.
  • Aoyagi Sōhonke Osu Honten: Traditional sweets — uiro cake and adorable frog-shaped red bean mochi.
  • Shin-Suzume Honten: A perpetually queued grilled dango shop; freshly grilled soy sauce and kinako flavors — perfect pocket-change street food.
  • Gin no An Osu Shopping Street: Popular croissant taiyaki and thin-skinned taiyaki.
  • Taiwan no Yaki-Manjū Bao-Bao-Tei: A queued snack — bite into juicy pan-fried pork buns.
  • Mukashi no Yabaton Osu Kannon-ten: Takeaway signature miso katsu skewers for a quick street-food fix.

Sakae Underground (Central Park) (18:20–21:00)

  • Transportation: From Osu (Kamimaezu / Osu Kannon Station) take the subway to Sakae Station, approx. 5–10 min.
  • Highlights: Nagoya's most developed underground mall network — shops and underground-exclusive dining, sheltered from the weather whatever the season.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Shisochō (始祖鳥): Highly recommended for dinner! 1-min walk from Fushimi Station — charcoal-grilled Mikawa Akadori skewers, all-private rooms. (Reservation: 21:00)
  • Suzunami Sakae Underground: A long-established shop marinating seafood in mirin and sake lees — sweet grilled fish set meals.
  • Kompal Sakae Underground: A local kissaten classic, known for its generously stuffed fried shrimp sandwich.

【Day 2】National Treasure Castle & Meiji Western Architecture

Inuyama Castle & Castle Town (09:40–13:00)

  • Transportation: Take Meitetsu Inuyama Line from Meitetsu Nagoya Station to Inuyama / Inuyamaen Station; express/limited express approx. 30–40 min.
  • Inuyama Castle: One of Japan's National Treasure castles, preserving the oldest wooden castle keep — climb to the top for views over the Kiso River.
  • Castle Town: Edo-period atmosphere with specialty snacks and century-old traditional townhouses, perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Sankō Inari Shrine: Famous for its "fortune multiplying" money-washing fountain, pink heart-shaped ema plaques, and rows of red torii gates.
  • Haritsuna Shrine: The guardian shrine of Inuyama Castle, enshrined for safe childbirth, longevity, and matchmaking.
  • Urakuen (National Treasure Tea House "Jo-an"): A refined Japanese garden housing one of Japan's three National Treasure tea houses — matcha experience available.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Kotobukihakoyu Inuyama Inoue-tei: Castle town must-eat — signature soy sauce rice cracker skewers.
  • Honmachi Saryō: Dengaku tofu skewers and goldfish jelly in a century-old traditional house.
  • Iseya Sunāoroshi: Long-established traditional restaurant — konnyaku dishes and Japanese kama-meshi clay pot rice.
  • Yoshikawaya: Seasonal fruit soft serve and parfaits — long queues during strawberry season.
  • Sakura Chaya: Grilled dango and tofu-shaped soft serve ice cream.
  • Inuyama Gyūtarō: Premium Hida beef nigiri sushi — melt-in-your-mouth bliss.

Meiji Mura (13:40–18:00)

  • Transportation: Take the Gifu Bus from Inuyama Station East Exit directly to Meiji Mura, approx. 20 min.
  • Highlights: A vast open-air architectural museum preserving Meiji-era Western buildings relocated from across Japan — rent retro clothing and ride the steam train.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Romantei: A romantically retro restaurant, famous for silky smooth omurice (omelette rice).
  • Gyūnabe Ōi Gyūniku-ten: Meiji-era "civilization beef" hot pot, using premium wagyu.

Dinner near Nagoya Station (19:00–21:00)

  • Transportation: Take the bus back to Inuyama Station from Meiji Mura, then Meitetsu back to Nagoya Station, approx. 60 min.
  • Backup: For a comfortable indoor dining option, head directly to the Esca Underground near the hotel.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Menya Hanabi Nagoya-eki-ten: The originator of Nagoya-style Taiwan mazemen — perfect garlic and ground pork combination.
  • Sekai no Yamachan Meieki Taikō-ten: (Near the hotel) Irresistibly bold pepper-seasoned tebasaki chicken wings.

【Day 3】Gifu: Warring States & Edo Charm

Nagoya to Gifu is extremely convenient — JR Rapid or Meitetsu Limited Express takes just 20–30 min. Upon arriving at Gifu Station, transfer to a city bus toward Gifu Park.

Mt. Kinka Ropeway + Squirrel Feeding + Gifu Castle (09:40–12:30)

  • Transportation: Take the train from Nagoya Station to Gifu Station (20–30 min), then take a city bus from the station to "Gifu Park Rekishi Hakubutsukan-mae" (15 min), and ride the ropeway up the mountain (3 min).
  • Mt. Kinka Ropeway: Head up early to enjoy sweeping views of the Nōbi Plain and the Nagara River.
  • Mt. Kinka Squirrel Village: Beside the mountaintop station — wear special gloves to hand-feed squirrels up close. Full of energy in the morning!
  • Gifu Castle: Oda Nobunaga's launchpad for "Tenka Fubu" (realm under his sword) — 360-degree panoramic views from the top.

Kawara-machi Old Town Lunch & Stroll (12:30–14:00)

  • Transportation: Take the ropeway back down to Gifu Park and walk a few minutes to Kawara-machi Old Town along the Nagara River.
  • Highlights: An Edo-era streetscape with rows of latticed machiya townhouses; traditional craft shops for Gifu wagasa parasols and paper lanterns line the street — allow about 1 hour for a relaxed browse.
Lunch Suggestions
  • Bunkaya: Japanese-Western fusion cuisine in a converted traditional house — enjoy a lunch set with a view of the Nagara River (popular times may require waiting).
  • Kawara-machi Izumiya: A renowned restaurant specializing in natural ayu sweetfish from the Nagara River.
  • Ikedo: A Western café in a century-old townhouse, serving Hida beef Japanese curry rice in a serene atmosphere.
  • Tamaiya Honpo: A Meiji-era century-old shop — ayu-shaped "Nobori Ayu" sweets make a perfect souvenir.

Noritake Garden (15:00–17:30)

Closed every Monday (moved to Tuesday if that falls on a public holiday); shops and museum inside usually open until 18:00, Craft Center admission until 16:30.

  • Transportation: At 14:00, take the bus from Gifu Park back to Gifu Station, then the train to Nagoya Station (approx. 60 min), walk 15 min; or take the subway to Kamejima Station and walk 5 min.
  • Highlights: The birthplace of premium bone china brand Noritake — vast lawns and red-brick factory buildings are perfect for photos. Aim to visit the museum before 16:30, then browse the porcelain shop.
Suggestions
  • Café Diamond Days: Refined afternoon tea served in premium Noritake porcelain.

AEON Mall Nagoya Noritake Garden Shopping & Dinner (17:30–21:00)

  • Transportation: Right next to Noritake Garden, 1-min walk; walk approx. 15 min back to Nagoya Station afterwards.
  • Highlights: A large shopping center that opened in late 2021, blending greenery with modern retail — one of the most enjoyable AEONs in the city.
  • TSUTAYA BOOKSTORE: A two-story towering book wall — a popular Nagoya Instagram landmark.
  • Fashion & Lifestyle: MUJI, DAISO (incl. THREEPPY / Standard Products), Uniqlo, GU, and more.
  • Fresh Supermarket: AEON Style on the first floor — great for buying late-night snacks, seasonal fruit, and supermarket treats.
Recommended Restaurants (In-Mall)
  • Kimi no Hamburg wo Tabetai (I Want to Eat Your Hamburg Steak): Clay-pot steamed rice with a juicy hamburger steak.
  • Hoshino Coffee: Signature oven-baked soufflé thick pancakes.
  • Kanazawa Maimon Sushi: Rotating sushi from Hokuriku Kanazawa — exceptionally high-quality seafood.
  • Buta-Michi: Well-known local pork restaurant — quality shabu-shabu or tonkatsu.
Backup (Nagoya Station Area)
  • Ajisen JR Nagoya-eki-ten: A popular local Chinese restaurant, famous for its fiery "Taiwan Ramen."
  • Sumiyaki Yakiniku Sugimoto Honten: A century-old meat purveyor serving Kuroge wagyu and Matsusaka beef.

【Day 4】Hida Little Kyoto & Traditional Villages

Today we take the JR Limited Express Hida to Takayama City — it's a long journey, so buy breakfast near Nagoya Station early and enjoy it on the train.

Takayama Old Town (Hida-Takayama) (10:30–14:00)

  • Transportation: From Nagoya Station, take the Limited Express Hida 1 (departs 07:43, arrives 10:16 — reserved seating) to Takayama Station, then walk 10–15 min to the old town.
  • Highlights: Known as "Little Kyoto of Hida," this well-preserved Edo-era wooden streetscape is lined with sake breweries, miso shops, and Hida beef snack stands — perfect for a stroll in kimono.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Hida Kitchen (Takayama KITchen): Highly recommended for lunch! Tender Hida beef steak. (Reservation: 12:30)
  • Hida Beef Sushi Sakaguchiya: A popular queue restaurant — top-grade Hida beef nigiri served on senbei crackers.
  • Rokujūban: Famous Hida beef grilled skewers, richly juicy.
  • Takayama Ramen Kikyōya: Local signature soy-sauce ramen with a clean, refreshing broth.

Hida Folk Village (Hida no Sato) (14:10–16:00)

  • Transportation: Take the Sarubobo Bus from the Nōhi Bus Center next to Takayama Station, approx. 10 min.
  • Highlights: An open-air museum with traditional Hida buildings relocated to one site — fewer crowds, an authentic village experience. Try making your own Hida sarubobo good-luck doll.
  • The park has a rest area where you can enjoy grilled dango or other refreshments.

Esca Underground Dinner near Nagoya Station (19:15–21:00)

  • Transportation: Return to Takayama Station by around 16:00, then take the Limited Express Hida 18 (departs 16:33, arrives 19:06 — reserved seating) back to Nagoya Station.
  • Highlights: Head straight to the Esca Underground near the hotel after returning to Nagoya Station — a well-deserved reward after a full day.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Yamamoto-ya Honten Esca-ten: Miso stew udon — firm noodles in a rich Hatcho miso broth, warming and restorative after a long day.
  • Toribiraki Sōhonke Esca-ten: Gold-award Nagoya Kōchin oyakodon — firm chicken, gloriously rich egg.

【Day 5】Traditional Tea Ceremony & Red-Brick Industrial Heritage

Check out at the hotel in the morning and leave luggage at the storage counter; collect it in the evening before heading to the airport.

Traditional Tea Ceremony (10:30–12:30)

  • Transportation: Near Kokusai Center Station / Nagoya Station — walk 10–15 min from Nagoya Station, or take the Sakura-dori subway line to Kokusai Center Station and walk 1 min.
  • Highlights: Led by a professional tea ceremony instructor — whisk your own matcha and pair it with Japanese wagashi sweets, experiencing the spirit of "wa-kei-sei-jaku" (harmony, respect, purity, tranquility).
  • Booking: Via Klook. (Reservation: 10:30)

Nagoya Station Area — Free Time & Final Shopping (14:00–16:00)

  • Highlights: Last two hours before departure — stock up on souvenirs or cosmetics. Browse Meitetsu Department Store, Takashimaya, Bic Camera, or the underground malls. Flexible and free.
Suggestions
  • Saijōen Matcha Shop: In the Nagoya Station area — matcha parfait to matcha cake, a must for matcha lovers. Don't miss it when walking through Meieki!
  • HARBS Nagoya Sakae Main / Meitetsu Nagoya: The birthplace of Japan's beloved fresh fruit mille-feuille crepe cake — the sweetest way to end the trip.

Chubu Centrair International Airport (17:30–20:00)

  • Transportation: Return to Nagoya Station to collect luggage, then take Meitetsu from Meitetsu Nagoya Station toward the airport (μ-SKY or regular limited express), approx. 35–45 min.
  • Highlights: Arriving early gives you time to visit the Flight of Dreams — a life-size Boeing 787 on display.
Recommended Restaurants / Snacks
  • Maruya Honten (Chubu Centrair): A perfect option for one last unagi eel rice bowl before boarding.
  • Yabaton (Chubu Centrair): One final taste of bold miso katsu before departure.
