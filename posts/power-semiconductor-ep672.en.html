II. Gooaye EP672 New / Updated Points (This Calibration · Assumed Entirely Correct)

EP672 does not overturn EP671 — it sharpens the "intensity, timeline, and winner profile" of the spillover effect. The following five points are the basis for this edition's thesis adjustments:

⏳ Speed Limiter Theory
Weak consumer electronics and auto demand ("dead weight"), which appear negative on any analysis sheet, are actually good for the AI power cycle: like a speed limiter, they prevent demand from spiking all at once → price collapse (demand destruction), allowing this hardware pricing cycle to run longer and further.
→ Cycle still in "early innings" — even a mid-cycle −30% correction does not change the long-term upward trend
📛 Lead Time "99+"
Western Tier-1 IDM premium power component lead times have stretched to over a year, with 99+ appearing (no delivery date whatsoever). Listener feedback (factory side): power components are tighter than passives in shortage severity.
→ A quantifiable supply/demand tightness indicator — useful supporting evidence for the thesis
🔁 Second-Source Qualification Surge
IDM capacity maxed out → customers forced to find second-source. Taiwan players are the "natural next choice," with significantly increased qualification and co-shipment opportunities in high-voltage MOSFETs / server MOSFETs.
→ Upgrade Taiwan IDM (Panjit/TW Semi) and leadframe order momentum scores
🏭 Capacity Is King
In a pricing upcycle, only companies that own capacity (IDM) or can secure foundry capacity (Fabless) win; without capacity, you can't ship even with orders. Gooaye noted: 6/8-inch foundry stocks showing relative strength (market positioning ahead of time).
→ Raise foundry capacity weighting (individual stock mapping per this report: Vanguard / Hana)
📊 Vishay Observation Indicator
Vishay has both passive and power businesses. A retrospective showed its power semiconductor lead time increased far more sharply than its passives — the key cross-market proof that "power is tighter than passives."
→ New external cross-validation data point added to the thesis
💧 Capital Rotation + Broad-Based Confirmation
Capital rotating from passive components (Yageo / high-end MLCC / inductors) into power semiconductors (ICs / MOSFETs); US, Japanese, and Chinese power stocks (e.g. Silan Micro) moving up collectively → a global rotation, which lends higher credibility (not a single-stock pump).
→ Institutional positioning confirms the trend is established
⚠️ EP672 Risk Reminders: ①Demand destruction — prices cannot rise indefinitely; if consumer end breaks (e.g. iPhone price-hike backlash), demand gets locked out; ②Single-stock pump — if it's one stock surging alone rather than a sector-wide move, watch for manipulation; ③Small Fabless with no capacity access — can't ship even with orders, attractiveness should be downgraded; ④Don't predict "which inning" — adaptability beats prophecy; react to price action signals.

V. Reading the Premise: Two Types of Industrial Cycle (PE Paradox × Speed Limiter)

To read valuation and "upside," first identify which type of cycle this industry belongs to — this determines whether PE is useful and how aggressively the market front-runs recovery.

DimensionDeep Commodity CycleMild Cycle
NatureSpot-price-driven commoditiesSpec-product / long-term contracts
Trough EarningsCan go negative (EPS turns negative)Remains positive throughout
Gross Margin SwingViolent (from negative to 40%+)Mild (~30%↔60%)
PE UsabilityBreaks down at inflection (trough PE explodes / goes negative)Still informative
Market Front-RunMost aggressive, earliestSmaller magnitude, more gradual
This CaseMemory · Passives (MLCC)Power / Analog IDM
⚠️ PE Paradox: Trailing PE for cyclical stocks can mislead in reverse — trough EPS low/negative → PE spikes, often the buy point; peak EPS high → PE looks cheap, often the sell point. Use forward PE and cross-check with Morningstar through-cycle fair values to see past the noise.
🟢 EP672 "Speed Limiter" Reinforcement: Weak consumer/auto demand suppresses the demand slope, so this power pricing wave won't spike to the top all at once (avoiding demand destruction and price collapse). For a mild cycle, this effectively pushes the "peak" further out and extends the cycle → front-run premium is spread over a longer horizon, "upside consumed" timeline must be viewed as multi-year, not ending with a single recovery year.

Implications for overseas IDMs in this report: Power / analog IDMs are a mild cycle (ON/STM/TXN never went negative at the trough), so forward PE is still valid; Morningstar through-cycle fair values already normalize for recovery, and ON's current price is still +35% above fair value → structural growth largely priced in. EP672 revision: Given the longer, extended cycle (99+ lead times, demand resilience), overseas IDMs' "expensiveness" is more likely to persist rather than imminently reverse — positioning remains as "leading indicator + demand resilience confirmer," with growth dimension score nudged up slightly (see Section X).
